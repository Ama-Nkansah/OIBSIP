A Tribute to

Whitney Houston

Timeless Music


Whitney Houston's voice was a gift from the heavens, a powerful, soulful, and angelic instrument that captivated the world.
With a career spanning decades, she left an indelible mark on music, inspiring generations with her iconic songs, unforgettable performances, and unwavering passion. From the soaring highs of "I Will Always Love You" to the upbeat joy of "I Wanna Dance with Somebody," her music was a testament to her talent, dedication, and resilience.
Though she may be gone, Whitney's legacy lives on, her voice continuing to uplift, inspire, and unite us all, a shining reminder of the transformative power of music and the enduring spirit of a true legend.




Some facts about her:

  • Born on August 9, 1963:
    • Whitney Elizabeth Houston was born in Newark, New Jersey.
  • Singing from a young age:
    • She began singing in church choirs and talent shows as a child.
  • Influenced by soul and gospel:
    • Her music was influenced by soul, gospel, and R&B legends like Aretha Franklin, Ella Fitzgerald, and Mahalia Jackson.
  • Discovered by Arista Records:
    • Whitney was discovered by Arista Records' Clive Davis, who signed her to a recording contract in 1983.
  • Debut album a huge success:
    • Her self-titled debut album (1985) sold over 25 million copies worldwide.
  • Record-breaking singles:
    • She had numerous #1 singles, including "Saving All My Love for You," "How Will I Know," and "I Will Always Love You."
  • Acting career:
    • Whitney appeared in films like "The Bodyguard" (1992), "Waiting to Exhale" (1995), and "The Preacher's Wife" (1996).
  • Awards and accolades:
    • She won 6 Grammy Awards, 2 Emmy Awards, and 30 Billboard Music Awards.
  • Vocal range:
    • Whitney's vocal range spanned over 3 octaves, showcasing her incredible vocal talent.
  • Legacy:
    • She remains one of the best-selling artists of all time, inspiring countless musicians and fans worldwide.
